How to Help Abandoned Roadside Pets

Essential guidelines for safely catching, treating, and fostering stray kittens and puppies.

1. Safety First

  • Do not chase: A frightened animal will run into traffic. Approach slowly, crouch down, and speak in calm, soft tones.
  • Lure with food: Use wet food, canned tuna, or dry kibble to coax the animal toward you rather than grabbing them.
  • Protect yourself: Wear thick gloves if possible. Strays might bite or scratch out of fear.

2. First Aid & Transport

  • Secure holding: Place strays in a well-ventilated cardboard box or pet carrier lined with towels.
  • Keep them warm: Stranded puppies and kittens (especially under 6 weeks) cannot regulate their temperature. Keep them wrapped up.
  • Offer water immediately: Roadside strays are almost always severely dehydrated. Offer water but avoid cow's milk.

3. Fostering & Feeding

  • Isolation: Keep rescued strays isolated from your own pets for at least 7 days to prevent disease transmission.
  • Kitten Feeding: Young kittens need warm specialized kitten formula (KMR). Do not feed them solid food if their eyes are closed.
  • Vet Check: Take them to a vet clinic as soon as possible for deworming, vaccines, and a flea treatment.
